## 6. Investor Relations Best Practices

### 10 Rules for World-Class Investor Relations

1. **Be proactive, not reactive.** Send updates before investors ask. Monthly cadence, no exceptions.

2. **Lead with bad news.** Mention challenges early in every update. Surprises destroy trust faster than bad news.

3. **Be specific with numbers.** "$48K new ARR" > "strong sales quarter." Data builds credibility.

4. **One clear ask per update.** Multiple asks get ignored. One specific ask (intro, advice, decision) gets action.

5. **Show what you learned.** Not just what happened, but what you learned and how you're adapting.

6. **Keep it brief.** Monthly updates: 800–1,200 words max. Respect investors' time.

7. **Celebrate investors' help.** Call out when an intro led to a deal or advice changed a decision.

8. **Document everything.** Board resolutions, approvals, and key decisions in writing — always.

9. **Build relationships outside meetings.** Grab coffee, send relevant articles, congratulate on their wins.

10. **Know your cap table.** Understand who holds what, pro-rata rights, and information rights at all times.

## 7. Investor Feedback Loop

### Collecting & Acting on Investor Input

**Monthly Feedback Collection**
- Reply-to-update format: investors reply with questions/thoughts
- CEO responds within 48 hours to every reply
- Log all feedback in CRM (HubSpot/Notion)

**Quarterly Feedback Survey** (5 questions, sent after board meeting)

```
1. How would you rate our transparency this quarter? (1–5)
2. What's your biggest concern about Justice OS right now?
3. Where do you think we're most underestimating an opportunity?
4. How helpful have you been able to be this quarter? (1–5)
5. One thing we should do differently next quarter?
```

**Feedback Review Process**
- CEO reviews all responses within 1 week
- Top themes presented at next board meeting
- Changes made and attributed: "Based on feedback from Q2, we changed X"

### Investor NPS

Track investor NPS twice per year:
- **Promoters (9–10):** Ask for referrals to other investors and customers
- **Passives (7–8):** Deepen relationship, identify gaps
- **Detractors (1–6):** Urgent 1:1 conversation — understand root cause

## 8. Follow-on Funding Strategy

### Series A Readiness Checklist

**Revenue & Growth**
- [ ] $1M+ ARR with clear path to $3M
- [ ] MoM growth rate ≥ 15% (past 6 months)
- [ ] Net Revenue Retention > 110%
- [ ] ≥ 10 paying enterprise customers

**Unit Economics**
- [ ] LTV:CAC ratio ≥ 3:1
- [ ] CAC payback period ≤ 18 months
- [ ] Gross margin ≥ 65%
- [ ] Documented path to profitability

**Product & Team**
- [ ] Core product feature-complete for ICP
- [ ] CTO / technical co-founder in place
- [ ] VP Sales with proven track record
- [ ] Customer NPS ≥ 50

**Storytelling & Materials**
- [ ] Updated pitch deck (12–15 slides)
- [ ] Data room complete (financials, legal, product)
- [ ] 3 compelling customer case studies
- [ ] Market sizing validated by third-party data

### Bridge Strategies (Pre-Series A)

| Option | Amount | Dilution | Timeline | Best For |
|--------|--------|----------|----------|---------|
| SAFE (uncapped) | $250K–$500K | TBD at Series A | 2–4 weeks | Existing investors |
| SAFE (capped) | $500K–$1.5M | 10–20% | 4–8 weeks | New angels |
| Revenue-based financing | $100K–$500K | 0% equity | 2–4 weeks | Post-revenue stage |
| Grant bridge | $50K–$250K | 0% | 3–6 months | Non-dilutive |
| Convertible note | $500K–$2M | 10–25% | 6–8 weeks | Institutional angels |
