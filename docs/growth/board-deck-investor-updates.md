## Monthly Update Guidelines

**Do:**
- Be honest, even when things are hard
- Use specific numbers, not vague language ("revenue grew" → "$12K MRR, up 18% MoM")
- Lead with the most important thing
- Acknowledge problems and explain what you're doing
- Make a specific ask (or explicitly say there is none)
- Keep it under 500 words

**Don't:**
- Spin bad news as good news
- Bury the lede — investors will read the first 3 sentences most carefully
- Send it late (builds trust to be consistent)
- Make the ask too big or vague ("any introductions would be helpful")
- Include more than 3–5 wins and 2 challenges

## Board Communication Best Practices

**Consistency builds trust:**
- Send monthly updates on the same day every month (no exceptions)
- Use the same format each time so investors know where to look
- If you miss a month, acknowledge it and explain why

**Honesty is non-negotiable:**
- Investors know startups are hard; what kills trust is surprises
- Bad news delivered early is better than bad news delivered late
- "We don't know yet" is acceptable; "everything is fine" when it isn't is not

**Make it actionable:**
- Every update should have a specific ask or "no ask this month"
- If you want an introduction, name the person specifically
- If you want advice, ask a specific question

**Brevity is a feature:**
- Monthly emails: 300–500 words maximum
- Quarterly decks: 10–15 slides maximum
- Annual decks: 15–20 slides maximum
- Investors are busy; respect their time
