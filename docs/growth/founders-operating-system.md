# Founder's Operating System (F-OS) — Justice OS

> **Decision-Making Framework + Templates for High-Performing Founders**

---

## Overview

The Founder's Operating System is a collection of recurring rituals, templates, and frameworks designed to help the Justice OS founding team operate with clarity, consistency, and momentum.

**Principles:**
- **Rituals over willpower** — Never rely on motivation alone
- **Metrics over feelings** — Let data guide decisions
- **People over process** — Optimize for team health
- **Focus over activity** — One big thing per week
- **Momentum over perfection** — Ship and learn

**Weekly Time Budget:**

| Activity | Hours/Week |
|----------|-----------|
| Deep work (product, writing, strategy) | 20 |
| Customer calls + demos | 8 |
| Team meetings + 1:1s | 4 |
| Community + partnerships | 4 |
| Planning + review rituals | 3 |
| Admin + email | 4 |
| Learning + reading | 3 |
| Buffer | 2 |
| **Total** | **48** |

## 1. Weekly Planning Ritual (Sunday, 60 min)

**When:** Sunday, 7–8 PM (before the week begins)
**Where:** Quiet space, no distractions
**Tools:** Notebook + calendar + task manager

**Step 1: Review Last Week (15 min)**

Review your weekly log from last Sunday:
- Did I accomplish the ONE big thing?
- What 3 things went well?
- What 1 thing should I do differently?
- Metrics check: MRR, pipeline, community activity

**Step 2: Set the Week's ONE Big Thing (5 min)**

> "If I could only accomplish one thing this week, what would have the biggest impact?"

Write it down. This is non-negotiable.

**Step 3: Plan the Week (20 min)**

For each day, answer:
- What is the MOST IMPORTANT task today (MIT)?
- What meetings or calls are scheduled?
- What could derail my focus? How will I handle it?

**Step 4: Anticipate Obstacles (10 min)**

- What is the single biggest risk this week?
- Who do I need to talk to?
- What decisions need to be made?
- What am I avoiding that I shouldn't be?

**Step 5: Energy + Mindset Check (10 min)**

- Rate your energy 1–10 entering this week
- What do you need to protect your energy?
- What is ONE thing you're grateful for?
- What is ONE thing you're excited about?

## 2. Daily Standup Template (15 min)

**When:** 8:00–8:15 AM daily (solo or with team)
**Format:** 3-question sprint

**Template:**
```
Date: ___________

YESTERDAY
- What did I complete? ___________
- Any blockers I hit? ___________

TODAY
- ONE most important task: ___________
- Supporting tasks (max 3): ___________
- Meetings/calls: ___________

BLOCKERS
- Current blockers: ___________
- Who can unblock me? ___________
- What do I need to decide? ___________

ENERGY (1–10): ___
```

**Rules:**
- Keep it to 15 minutes maximum
- If with a team, each person gets 3 minutes
- Blockers get addressed AFTER standup, not during
- No status updates — only commitments and blockers

## 5. Quarterly Planning (Goals + OKRs, Full Day)

**When:** First Monday of each quarter (8 AM–4 PM)
**Participants:** Founders + key team members
**Prep:** Each person reviews last quarter's OKRs + writes 3 proposed priorities

**Agenda:**

| Time | Activity |
|------|---------|
| 8:00–9:00 AM | Last quarter retrospective |
| 9:00–10:30 AM | Market + competitive review |
| 10:30 AM–12:00 PM | Set quarterly OKRs (3 objectives max) |
| 12:00–1:00 PM | Lunch + informal discussion |
| 1:00–2:00 PM | Headcount + resource plan |
| 2:00–3:00 PM | Risk register update |
| 3:00–4:00 PM | Communicate plan to team + investors |

**OKR Format:**
```
Quarter: Q___ / Year: ___

OBJECTIVE 1: [1-sentence ambitious goal]
  KR 1.1: [Measurable outcome] — Target: ___
  KR 1.2: [Measurable outcome] — Target: ___
  KR 1.3: [Measurable outcome] — Target: ___

OBJECTIVE 2: [1-sentence ambitious goal]
  KR 2.1: [Measurable outcome] — Target: ___
  KR 2.2: [Measurable outcome] — Target: ___

OBJECTIVE 3: [1-sentence ambitious goal]
  KR 3.1: [Measurable outcome] — Target: ___
  KR 3.2: [Measurable outcome] — Target: ___

RULES:
- Objectives are inspirational, not measurable
- Key Results are measurable and time-bound
- 60–70% completion = good (100% = too easy)
- 3 objectives max; avoid spreading too thin
```

## 6. Annual Planning (Vision + Roadmap, 2 Days)

**When:** December / January
**Format:** Off-site (or intensive at-home)

**Day 1: Review + Reflect**
- Morning: Year in review (metrics, wins, pivots, losses)
- Afternoon: Personal reflection (what energized me? what drained me?)
- Evening: Team dinner + celebration

**Day 2: Plan + Commit**
- Morning: Set annual vision and 3 big bets
- Afternoon: Build roadmap + milestones
- Evening: Share plan with team

**Annual Vision Template:**

**Agenda (employee leads):**
- 10 min: What's on their mind (personal + professional)
- 10 min: Project updates + blockers
- 10 min: Career development + feedback

**Rules:** Employee sets agenda. Manager listens.

```
ON MY MIND

- [Project 1]: Status | Blocker
- [Project 2]: Status | Blocker

FEEDBACK WANTED
- I'd like feedback on: ___________

SUPPORT NEEDED
- I need help with: ___________
```

### 7.2 Team Weekly Sync (Monday, 30 min)

**Agenda:**
- 5 min: Check-in round (energy, 1 word)
- 10 min: Progress on OKRs (go-around)
- 10 min: Blockers + cross-team dependencies
- 5 min: Wins + shoutouts

### 7.3 Board / Investor Prep (Monthly, 60 min)

**Agenda:**
- 15 min: Review metrics deck
- 15 min: Review major decisions + rationale
- 15 min: Anticipated questions + responses
- 15 min: Ask / support needed from board
