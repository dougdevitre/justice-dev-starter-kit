# Scaling Checklist — Milestone-Based Criteria — Justice OS

> **Criteria for When and How to Scale Different Functions**

---

## Overview

Scaling too fast is as dangerous as scaling too slow. This checklist provides clear, metrics-based criteria for when to hire, expand product lines, enter new geographies, and grow the team.

**Core Rule:** Don't hire ahead of revenue. Hire to capture revenue that is already within reach.

---

## When to Hire

### Developer Advocate — Hire at $100K ARR

**Signal:** You have users but not enough developers building on the platform.

**Criteria to trigger hire:**
- [ ] ARR ≥ $100K
- [ ] 50+ developers have used the API
- [ ] GitHub stars growing at < 15%/month (demand for help)
- [ ] Monthly community call attendance > 30
- [ ] Founders spending > 5 hrs/week answering developer questions

**What this person does:**
- Creates tutorials, sample apps, and documentation
- Speaks at developer conferences and meetups
- Responds to developer questions on GitHub, Discord, Stack Overflow
- Runs monthly developer office hours
- Maintains the 72-project ecosystem

**Hiring Profile:**
- Software developer with 3+ years experience
- Track record of open-source contributions
- Communication skills: can write tutorials, give talks, teach
- Passion for access to justice
- Salary range: $90K–$130K + equity

---

### Marketing Manager — Hire at $250K ARR

**Signal:** Content and outreach are generating results but you can't keep up with the volume.

**Criteria to trigger hire:**
- [ ] ARR ≥ $250K
- [ ] Founder spending > 8 hrs/week on content and marketing
- [ ] Inbound leads growing (more than 10/month) but unmanaged
- [ ] Email list > 1,000 subscribers
- [ ] At least 3 proven content formats (blog, newsletter, social)

**What this person does:**
- Manages content calendar and publishing
- Runs email campaigns and drip sequences
- Manages social media (LinkedIn, Twitter)
- Coordinates conference speaking and PR
- Reports on marketing metrics weekly

**Hiring Profile:**
- B2B SaaS marketing experience
- Understands content, SEO, email, and events
- Social impact or nonprofit sector knowledge a plus
- Data-driven; comfortable with analytics tools
- Salary range: $85K–$120K + equity

---

### Sales Person — Hire at $500K ARR

**Signal:** Founder can no longer personally close all deals in the pipeline.

**Criteria to trigger hire:**
- [ ] ARR ≥ $500K
- [ ] MRR growing > 10%/month for 3+ consecutive months
- [ ] Founder spending > 12 hrs/week on sales activities
- [ ] Pipeline value > $300K (3-month forward view)
- [ ] Average deal size > $5,000/yr (worth a salesperson's time)
- [ ] Sales cycle documented and repeatable

**What this person does:**
- Owns the full sales cycle (qualification to close)
- Manages CRM (Pipedrive/HubSpot)
- Conducts product demos
- Writes proposals and negotiates contracts
- Works with CSM on upsell opportunities

**Hiring Profile:**
- B2B SaaS sales experience (2–5 years)
- Proven track record of closing $10K–$100K deals
- Legal tech, nonprofit, or government sales a plus
- Self-directed; can build a pipeline from scratch
- Salary range: $70K–$100K base + OTE $140K–$200K

---

### Customer Success Manager — Hire at 50 Paying Customers

**Signal:** You can no longer personally manage all customer relationships.

**Criteria to trigger hire:**
- [ ] 50+ paying customers
- [ ] Churn rate > 2%/month (need dedicated retention)
- [ ] Onboarding is inconsistent (no repeatable process yet)
- [ ] Founder spending > 6 hrs/week on customer support and check-ins
- [ ] NPS < 50 (need active management to improve)

**What this person does:**
- Manages onboarding for all new customers
- Conducts monthly business reviews
- Tracks health scores and flags at-risk accounts
- Drives product adoption and expansion revenue
- Collects customer feedback for product team

**Hiring Profile:**
- Customer success experience in B2B SaaS
- Strong empathy and communication skills
- Technical enough to troubleshoot issues
- Data-driven: comfortable with metrics and dashboards
- Legal tech or nonprofit domain knowledge a plus
- Salary range: $75K–$110K + equity

---

### Operations Person — Hire at $1M ARR

**Signal:** Founder is spending too much time on non-revenue-generating operational tasks.

**Criteria to trigger hire:**
- [ ] ARR ≥ $1M
- [ ] Founder spending > 8 hrs/week on operations, finance, HR, and admin
- [ ] Team size ≥ 6 people (coordination overhead is real)
- [ ] Vendor contracts, compliance, and tooling becoming complex

**What this person does:**
- Manages HR processes (onboarding, payroll, benefits)
- Oversees finance (bookkeeping, accounts payable, financial reporting)
- Manages vendor relationships and tool stack
- Implements operational playbooks across teams
- Supports recruiting process

**Hiring Profile:**
- Operations generalist with startup experience
- Process-oriented: likes building systems from scratch
- Financial literacy (can read a P&L, manage budgets)
- Tools: spreadsheets, Notion/Linear, Gusto/Rippling
- Salary range: $80K–$120K + equity

## When to Expand

### Geographic Expansion — 3 US Regions Established

**Trigger:** You have customers in 3 or more US regions (e.g., Northeast, Midwest, Southeast) and are seeing organic interest from a 4th.

**Pre-expansion checklist:**
- [ ] Core product is feature-complete for target use cases
- [ ] Sales and CS playbooks are documented and repeatable
- [ ] At least 1 reference customer per region
- [ ] Legal and compliance review for new jurisdiction requirements
- [ ] Conference presence in target region (at least 1 talk delivered)

**Expansion Approach:**
1. Hire a regional sales/partnerships lead
2. Identify 5 anchor partners in the new region
3. Attend 2 regional conferences in first year
4. Set 12-month target: $250K ARR in new region

---

### Product Line Expansion — 5 Case Types → 20+

**Trigger:** Core case types (e.g., housing, family, immigration) are working well and customers are requesting new case types.

**Pre-expansion checklist:**
- [ ] Existing case types have 90%+ customer satisfaction
- [ ] At least 3 customers have requested the new case type
- [ ] Technical architecture supports new case types without major refactoring
- [ ] Developer resources available (at least 1 engineer dedicated)
- [ ] Content and documentation can be produced for new case type

**Case Type Expansion Priority Order:**
1. Housing (core) → Family → Immigration (Phase 1)
2. Criminal defense → Small claims → Employment (Phase 2)
3. Elder law → Disability rights → Consumer protection (Phase 3)
4. Environmental → Business formation → Tax (Phase 4)
5. International → Asylum → Juvenile (Phase 5)

### Team Doubling — Each $1M ARR Increment

**Rule of thumb:** Double the team for each $1M ARR milestone.

| ARR Milestone | Team Size Target |

| $250K | 3–4 |
| $500K | 5–6 |
| $1M | 8–10 |
| $2M | 14–18 |
| $5M | 30–40 |
| $10M | 60–80 |
| $20M | 100–130 |

| $3M | 8 | 4 | 2 | 2 | 1 |

### International Expansion — 3 Countries

**Trigger:** US market is well-established ($3M+ ARR) and you have inbound demand from international markets.

**Pre-expansion checklist:**
- [ ] ARR ≥ $3M (US market)
- [ ] Inbound inquiries from target countries (at least 3/month)
- [ ] Product localization feasibility assessed
- [ ] Legal and compliance requirements reviewed
- [ ] Partnership opportunities identified in target country
- [ ] Language support plan (UI, documentation, support)

**Priority Countries for Expansion:**
1. **Canada** — Similar legal system, same language, strong legal aid ecosystem
2. **United Kingdom** — Common law system, large legal aid sector, strong legal tech community
3. **Australia** — Similar legal system, growing legal tech ecosystem

**International Expansion Model:**
- Start with distribution partners (not direct sales)
- Partner with local legal aid organizations for credibility
- Localize product (currency, court terminology, jurisdiction data)
- Consider local entity for government contracts

## Scaling Decision Framework

When evaluating any scaling decision, ask:

| Question | Green Light | Red Light |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Do we have the revenue to support this? | Yes, with 3 months buffer | No, would require fundraising |
| Is this the highest-leverage action? | Yes, directly impacts core goal | No, there are higher-priority options |
| Do we have process documented? | Yes, playbook exists | No, still figuring it out |
| Is the team ready? | Yes, capacity and skills exist | No, current team is at max |
| What happens if we're wrong? | Recoverable within 30–60 days | Catastrophic / hard to reverse |

**Decision Rule:** Need ≥ 4 green lights to proceed. If any red lights remain, address them first or wait for the next milestone.
