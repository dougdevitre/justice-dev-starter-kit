## 1. Brand Mission & Values

### Mission Statement

> **"Justice OS exists to make legal help accessible to everyone, regardless of income."**

This sentence is not a marketing line. It is the lens through which every product decision, partnership, and brand expression is evaluated. When the mission is this clear, every brand choice becomes easier: Does this feel accessible? Does this feel like it's for everyone? Does this communicate that the legal system is not just for the wealthy?

### Vision Statement

> **"A world where no one navigates the legal system alone."**

The vision is the world we are building toward. It is emotionally resonant because the loneliness of facing the legal system — without a lawyer, without understanding, without a guide — is one of the most disempowering experiences a person can have. Justice OS ends that loneliness with technology, community, and open-source infrastructure.

---

### Core Values

#### 1. Radical Accessibility
*"Legal help should cost what people can afford to pay."*

We believe that the legal system is a public institution, and the tools for navigating it should not be priced for Wall Street law firms. Radical accessibility means our free tier is genuinely functional — not a watered-down demo. It means we design for low-bandwidth internet connections and low-end smartphones. It means plain language, not legal language, in every interface. Accessibility is not a feature we add after the fact — it is the founding principle of everything we build.

#### 2. Open by Default
*"Open source as a moral stance, not just a development model."*

We open-source our code because legal infrastructure should not be locked in proprietary systems owned by shareholders with no stake in justice. Open by default means our API is public. Our roadmap is public. Our governance is public. We believe transparency in technology is the same kind of transparency we demand in courts — and for the same reason. Openness builds trust, and trust is the foundation of justice.

#### 3. Community First
*"Built with the communities we serve, not for them."*

Software built for low-income legal aid clients without involving them in the design process will fail those clients. Community First means that every major feature begins with user research that includes self-represented litigants, not just legal aid attorneys. It means our contributor community includes people with lived experience of the justice gap. It means we fund community translation, not just professional translation. We are guests in the communities we serve, and we behave accordingly.

#### 4. Human Dignity
*"Every interaction should respect the full humanity of each user."*

People who come to Justice OS are often in the worst moments of their lives: facing eviction, fleeing domestic violence, fighting for custody of their children, or challenging wrongful debt collection. Every word of copy, every form field label, every error message, and every notification carries the weight of that context. Human Dignity means we design as if the person on the other side of the screen is a full human being in a difficult moment — not a user ID to be processed.

#### 5. Rigorous Impact
*"We measure what matters and hold ourselves accountable."*

Mission-driven technology that cannot measure its impact is indistinguishable from technology that has no impact. Rigorous Impact means we track the metrics that matter: cases resolved, clients served, documents filed, time saved, access expanded. We publish our impact data publicly. We hold ourselves accountable to outcomes, not outputs. And when our data shows we are not helping people, we change course — no matter how much we love the feature.

## 2. Visual Identity

### Logo Concept

The Justice OS logo reimagines the traditional scales of justice as an **open network constellation** — a visual metaphor for the transformation from a closed, inaccessible legal system to an interconnected, open infrastructure for justice.

The two traditional scale pans become **connected nodes** in a network graph — each node representing a person, an organization, or a court. Rather than one side being weighed against another (winner vs. loser), the nodes are in balance as an ecosystem, connected by lines that suggest communication, access, and shared infrastructure. A subtle "OS" monogram is integrated into the network pattern, reinforcing the platform identity.

**Logo Usage:**
- Full logo: wordmark "Justice OS" beside the network constellation mark
- Icon only: constellation mark for app icons, favicons, and small-format uses
- Wordmark only: for text-heavy contexts where the icon would compete
- Minimum size: 24px height for icon, 80px height for full logo
- Clear space: equal to the height of the "J" in "Justice" on all sides

## 3. Tone of Voice Guide

### Brand Personality in Communication

Justice OS communicates with five defining qualities:

1. **Clear** — We never use jargon when plain language will do. We write for a 7th-grade reading level in user-facing copy.
2. **Empowering** — We tell people what they can do, not just what the system requires of them.
3. **Warm** — We are not a government form. We are a guide. We write as if we care about the person reading.
4. **Precise** — Legal technology requires accuracy. We are warm and precise — warmth does not mean vague.
5. **Courageous** — We say things clearly even when they are hard. We do not soften bad news with corporate language.

---

### Do / Don't by Communication Context

#### User-Facing Product Copy
| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|---|---|
| "Your petition is ready to review." | "Your document has been generated successfully." |
| "It looks like you haven't filed yet. Need help?" | "Filing status: Pending. Action Required." |
| "You have 3 days to respond — here's what to do." | "Response deadline in 72 hours. See documentation." |

#### Legal Aid Partner Communications
| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|---|---|
| "Your team is doing incredible work — here's what your data shows." | "User metrics indicate increased platform utilization." |
| "We noticed intake is taking longer than average — want to troubleshoot?" | "Anomalous intake duration detected. Review recommended." |
| "Here's your 30-day outcomes summary." | "Please find attached your monthly utilization report." |

#### Developer Documentation
| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|---|---|
| "To create a case, POST to /api/v1/cases with this JSON body:" | "The case creation endpoint accepts a JSON payload conforming to the CaseCreateRequest schema." |
| "If you get a 403, check your JWT token — it may have expired." | "Authentication errors may indicate token validation failures." |
| "Here's a working example you can run right now:" | "The following code snippet illustrates the pattern:" |

#### Social Media
| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|---|---|
| "A single mother in Ohio just filed her first eviction defense using Justice OS. 🏠" | "Justice OS platform facilitates civil legal aid document automation." |
| "Open source legal tech > $500/hour legal fees." | "Justice OS provides cost-effective alternatives to traditional legal representation." |
| "We shipped offline mode today. Because not everyone has WiFi in the courtroom waiting room." | "New feature release: Service Worker offline caching now available in PWA." |

#### Press & Media
| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|---|---|
| "We're building the operating system for equal justice." | "We provide an integrated legal case management platform." |
| "Every day, 80% of people with civil legal problems get no help. We're fixing that." | "We address the unmet civil legal need in the United States." |
| "Justice OS is open source because the law belongs to everyone." | "Our open-source model enables community-driven development." |

### Sample Rewrites — Before & After

**Before (legalese/cold):**
> "The application for fee waiver must be submitted concurrently with the initiating pleading or no later than the date of first appearance pursuant to CRC Rule 3.50."

**After (Justice OS voice):**
> "You can apply to have your filing fee waived. Submit your fee waiver application at the same time you file your first document. Here's the form — it takes about 10 minutes to complete."

---

**Before (cold/corporate):**
> "Your account has been provisioned. Initial configuration requires administrator action to enable functionality."

**After (Justice OS voice):**
> "Welcome to Justice OS! Your account is ready. Your admin needs to finish a few settings before your team can start — here's a quick guide to get them there."

---

**Before (intimidating):**
> "Failure to respond to this complaint within the statutory period may result in entry of default judgment against you."

**After (Justice OS voice):**
> "You received a legal complaint and need to respond. You have [X days] to respond — if you don't, the other side may win automatically. Here's how to respond, even without a lawyer."

## 5. Brand Personality

### Trait 1: Champion

**Description:** Justice OS is a champion for the people it serves — the intake worker who processes 40 clients per day, the single parent facing eviction, the attorney doing pro bono work on top of a full caseload. A champion is bold, specific, and unafraid to name injustice directly. Champions don't hedge.

**In Product Copy:** "You responded to the complaint on time. That was the hard part."

**In Marketing Copy:** "50 million Americans face civil legal problems without a lawyer every year. We're building the technology to change that number."

---

### Trait 2: Guide

**Description:** A champion fights for you; a guide walks alongside you. Justice OS is a guide for users who are navigating territory they've never seen before. Guides are calm, clear, and reassuring. They don't panic. They don't overwhelm. They say "here's the next step" — and mean it.

**In Product Copy:** "You're almost there. Review your document one more time, then we'll help you file it."

**In Marketing Copy:** "From the first intake call to the last court date, Justice OS guides your team through every step of the case."

---

### Trait 3: Innovator

**Description:** Justice OS is technically ambitious. We are building AI-powered document analysis, guided interview engines, and predictive case management tools. The Innovator personality shows up when we talk about technology — with excitement that is grounded and specific, not buzzword-driven.

**In Product Copy:** "AI analyzed your lease and flagged 3 unusual clauses. Here's what they mean in plain language."

**In Marketing Copy:** "We built an AI that reads leases the way a housing attorney reads leases — so your clients have that expertise without the hourly rate."

---

### Trait 4: Community Builder

**Description:** Justice OS is not built by a company for users. It is built by a community for a community. The Community Builder personality is warm, collaborative, and inclusive — it invites participation rather than pitching at an audience.

**In Product Copy:** "Suggest an improvement — your feedback shapes the next version."

**In Marketing Copy:** "Justice OS is built by legal aid workers, civic technologists, and open-source developers who believe the legal system should work for everyone. You're invited."

---

### Trait 5: Truth-Teller

**Description:** The legal system is complicated, slow, and often unjust. Justice OS does not pretend otherwise. The Truth-Teller trait shows up in honest communication about what the platform can and cannot do, realistic expectations about legal outcomes, and a refusal to use euphemistic corporate language when plain truth is available.

**In Product Copy:** "This document helps — but talking to a lawyer is always better when it's possible. Here's how to find free legal help near you."

**In Marketing Copy:** "We can't fix the legal system. But we can make sure more people have the tools to navigate it."

## 8. Taglines

### Five Tagline Options

**1. "Justice, Open to All"**
**Rationale:** Simple, clear, and dual-meaning — open source AND open access. Connects the technical (open source) to the mission (open to all). Works for all audiences. Slightly aspirational without being grandiose.

**2. "The Operating System for Equal Justice"**
**Rationale:** Owns the "OS" brand element explicitly. Positions Justice OS as infrastructure — not a feature or an app, but the platform layer that everything else runs on. Resonates strongly with technologists and systems thinkers. May be too abstract for self-represented litigants.

**3. "Legal Help. Human Scale."**
**Rationale:** Speaks to accessibility and the human dimension simultaneously. "Human Scale" contrasts with the overwhelming, institutional scale of the legal system. Emotional and precise. Works well for legal aid and SRL audiences. May under-communicate the technology element.

**4. "Built for Justice. Open for Everyone."**
**Rationale:** Emphasizes both the mission (built for justice) and the access philosophy (open for everyone, including open source). The parallel structure makes it memorable and quotable. Comprehensive audience appeal.

**5. "Where Law Meets Open Source"**
**Rationale:** Clear, direct, and informative for developer and civic tech audiences. Accurately describes the intersection. Less emotionally resonant for SRL or legal aid audiences who may not know or care about "open source."

---

### Recommended Primary Tagline

**"Justice, Open to All"**

This tagline is recommended as the primary brand tagline because it:
1. **Works across all audiences** — legal aid professionals, courts, self-represented litigants, and developers all find it resonant
2. **Embeds the brand name meaning** — "Open" refers to open source (for technical audiences) and open access (for everyone else) simultaneously
3. **Is emotionally true** — It states the mission in three words without corporate abstraction
4. **Is defensible** — No competitor can credibly claim this line; it is specific to Justice OS's positioning
5. **Is brief** — Three words; easy to remember, easy to say, easy to put on a t-shirt at a conference

**Secondary taglines** recommended for specific contexts:
- Developer/Technical audiences: "The Operating System for Equal Justice"
- Legal Aid audiences: "Built for Justice. Open for Everyone."
- Marketing campaigns: "Legal Help. Human Scale."

## 9. Brand Evolution Plan

### Phase 1: "The Credibility Phase" (Year 1)

**Objective:** Establish Justice OS as a serious, trustworthy platform among legal professionals.

**Strategy:** Legal professionals are skeptical of new technology, and especially skeptical of free, open-source technology. The Year 1 brand strategy is entirely focused on building credibility with this audience.

**Brand Activities:**
- Publish detailed technical documentation and security practices
- Secure endorsements from respected legal aid executive directors and judges
- Present at ABA TECHSHOW, NLADA Annual Conference, and Legal Services Corporation Technology Summit
- Publish quarterly impact reports with real outcome data
- Partner with 3–5 flagship legal aid organizations for co-branded case studies
- Tone: authoritative, precise, evidence-based

**Key Brand Touchpoints Year 1:**
- Website: credibility-focused (customer logos, case studies, security certifications)
- GitHub: well-maintained, documentation-rich, active issue engagement
- Conferences: booth + speaking slots; priority: legal aid and court technology events

---

### Phase 2: "The Scale Phase" (Year 2–3)

**Objective:** Build brand recognition as the category leader in access-to-justice technology.

**Strategy:** With credibility established, expand brand presence to reach more audiences — courts, government agencies, law schools, and the broader civic technology community.

**Brand Activities:**
- Launch the Justice OS user conference (virtual Year 2, hybrid Year 3)
- Expand press relations: target The American Lawyer, Law360, TechCrunch (civic tech coverage), Nonprofit Quarterly
- Launch developer marketing: blog, podcast appearances, conference sponsorships at ReactConf, PyCon
- Introduce customer advocacy program: Justice OS Champions (customers who speak publicly on our behalf)
- Build brand presence at law school clinics: student competitions, curriculum integration
- Tone: inspiring, community-oriented, proof-driven

**Key Brand Touchpoints Year 2–3:**
- Annual impact report (professionally designed, widely distributed)
- Customer stories video series: short-form documentary about clients served
- Developer podcast appearances and technical blog posts
- Law school partnership program with named sponsorships

---

### Phase 3: "The Movement Phase" (Year 3+)

**Objective:** Make Justice OS synonymous with the access-to-justice movement — the brand that represents the belief that the legal system should work for everyone.

**Strategy:** Phase 3 is not about selling software. It is about cementing Justice OS as a symbol — the way Linux became the symbol of open-source infrastructure, or Mozilla Firefox became the symbol of the open web. Organizations that use Justice OS should feel that they are part of something larger than their own caseload.

**Brand Activities:**
- Launch the Justice OS Foundation (separate nonprofit entity) to steward the open-source project
- Publish the annual "State of Justice Technology" report — the definitive data on technology's role in access to justice
- Commission original research on the justice gap and technology's potential role
- Launch the Justice OS Fellowship — paid fellowships for developers and designers from underrepresented communities to contribute full-time to the project
- Build coalition of 500+ legal aid organizations publicly committing to open-source justice infrastructure
- Tone: movement-oriented, bold, historic

**Key Brand Touchpoints Year 3+:**
- Justice OS Foundation website and annual report
- Fellowship program as brand asset and talent pipeline
- Coalition member badges and co-marketing with member organizations
- State of Justice Technology report as thought-leadership anchor
- Justice OS as the default recommendation by legal aid funders and accreditation bodies
