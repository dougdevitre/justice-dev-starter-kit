## 2. OKR Setting Framework

### Company-Level OKR Template

```
OBJECTIVE: [Inspiring, qualitative goal — what we want to achieve]
Quarter: Q[X] [Year]
Owner: [Name/Team]

KEY RESULT 1: [Measurable outcome — how we know we achieved it]
  Baseline: [Current state]
  Target: [End-of-quarter target]
  Data Source: [Where this is measured]
  Confidence: [H/M/L at start of quarter]

KEY RESULT 2: [Measurable outcome]
  Baseline: ___
  Target: ___
  Data Source: ___
  Confidence: ___

KEY RESULT 3: [Measurable outcome]
  Baseline: ___
  Target: ___
  Data Source: ___
  Confidence: ___
```

### Example: Q2 2026

**Objective:** Establish Justice OS as the leading open-source legal tech platform for legal aid organizations.

| Key Result | Baseline | Target | Owner |
|------------|----------|--------|-------|
| Active legal aid org deployments | 2 | 10 | CS Team |
| GitHub stars | 500 | 1,500 | Community |
| API documentation coverage | 40% | 90% | Engineering |
| NPS from legal aid partners | 32 | 50 | CS Team |

### Scoring Guide

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0.0–0.3 | Did not make meaningful progress |
| 0.4–0.6 | Made progress but fell short |
| 0.7–1.0 | Achieved the key result |
| > 1.0 | Exceeded (re-calibrate baseline next quarter) |

> **Target:** Average OKR score of 0.7. Consistently scoring 1.0 means goals are too easy.

## 3. Priority Mapping Tool (2×2 Matrix)

### Impact vs. Effort Matrix

```
HIGH IMPACT
     |
     |  QUICK WINS        |  STRATEGIC BETS
     |  (Do First)        |  (Schedule & Plan)
     |                    |
     |--------------------|--------------------
     |                    |
     |  FILL-INS          |  THANKLESS TASKS
     |  (Do if time)      |  (Eliminate/Delegate)
     |                    |
LOW IMPACT
     LOW EFFORT ---------> HIGH EFFORT
```

### Priority Mapping Worksheet

| Initiative | Impact (1-5) | Effort (1-5) | Score (I/E) | Quadrant | Decision |
|------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|----------|---------|
| [Initiative 1] | ___ | ___ | ___ | ___ | ___ |
| [Initiative 2] | ___ | ___ | ___ | ___ | ___ |
| [Initiative 3] | ___ | ___ | ___ | ___ | ___ |
| [Initiative 4] | ___ | ___ | ___ | ___ | ___ |
| [Initiative 5] | ___ | ___ | ___ | ___ | ___ |

### Decision Rules
- **Score ≥ 3.0:** High priority — plan into current quarter
- **Score 1.5–2.9:** Medium priority — backlog for next quarter
- **Score < 1.5:** Low priority — eliminate or delegate

## 6. Weekly Standup Template

### Async Version (Slack/Loom — Monday by 10AM)

```
🗓️ WEEK OF [DATE] — [NAME]

✅ DONE LAST WEEK:
- [Accomplishment 1]
- [Accomplishment 2]
- [Accomplishment 3]

🎯 DOING THIS WEEK:
- [Priority 1] — tied to [OKR]
- [Priority 2] — tied to [OKR]
- [Priority 3] — tied to [OKR]

🚧 BLOCKERS:
- [Blocker 1] — need [from whom] by [when]
- [None]

📊 KEY METRIC UPDATE:
- [Your primary metric]: [value] (target: [value])
```

### Sync Version (15-min, Tues/Thurs)

| Slot | Time | Content |
|------|------|---------|
| Round-robin updates | 8 min | Each person: done/doing/blocked (60 sec each) |
| Blocker resolution | 5 min | Address blockers raised |
| Shout-outs | 2 min | Recognize wins |

**Rules:**
- No status updates — those go async
- Surface blockers only — solve offline
- Keep to 15 min strictly

## 7. Monthly Business Review Template

### MBR Deck Outline (5 Slides)

**Slide 1: Scorecard**
- OKR scores (current quarter progress)
- Top 5 KPIs vs. targets (green/yellow/red)
- One headline: "Month in one sentence"

**Slide 2: Revenue & Growth**
- MRR / ARR chart (trailing 6 months)
- New customers, churn, expansion revenue
- Pipeline summary (deals by stage)

**Slide 3: Product & Operations**
- Features shipped vs. planned
- System uptime & incident summary
- Support ticket resolution rates

**Slide 4: Team & Culture**
- Headcount (current vs. plan)
- Open roles & hiring progress
- Team health pulse (1–5 scale)

**Slide 5: Next Month Priorities**
- Top 3 company priorities
- Key decisions needed
- Risks to watch
