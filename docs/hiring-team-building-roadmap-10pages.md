## 1. Hiring Philosophy

Justice OS hires people who are building a movement, not just a product. Every hiring decision is a statement about our values, our commitment to our mission, and the kind of team we want to be.

### Mission-Driven Talent First

We do not hire the best engineer who happens to apply. We hire the best engineer who is genuinely motivated by access-to-justice. The two are not always the same person. Our hiring process is designed to find people for whom this work is personal — not as a requirement, but because those people do their best work here and stay the longest.

### Diversity as a Strength

We commit to building a team that reflects the communities we serve. Legal aid clients are disproportionately low-income, people of color, immigrants, and people with disabilities. A team that does not include people with lived experience of navigating the legal system will build a product that does not serve those communities well. Diversity is not a compliance goal — it is a product quality requirement.

### Remote-First, Asynchronous-Default

Justice OS is a remote-first organization. This is not a cost-cutting measure — it is a talent strategy. The best mission-driven technologists are not concentrated in any one city. Remote-first also makes us more equitable: we do not require candidates to relocate, which disproportionately excludes caregivers and candidates from lower-income backgrounds.

### Compensation Transparency

We publish salary bands publicly. We do not negotiate compensation based on what a candidate is currently earning (a practice that perpetuates pay inequality). We negotiate based on the value of the role and our published bands. All salary decisions are documented and reviewed for pay equity every 6 months.

## 4. Month 0–3: Full-Stack Engineer

### Job Description
Justice OS is seeking a mission-driven Full-Stack Engineer to help build the open-source legal technology platform that powers access to justice for millions of Americans. You will be the first technical hire, working directly with the founder to architect and ship the core platform — from authentication and case management to document assembly and public APIs. You believe technology can close the justice gap, and you want your code to prove it.

### Responsibilities
- Architect and implement the Justice OS core platform, including the legal data model, REST API, and case management UI
- Build multi-tenant infrastructure that scales from 3 organizations to 3,000 with minimal re-architecting
- Implement security best practices: authentication, authorization, encryption at rest and in transit, and audit logging
- Contribute to API design decisions and write the OpenAPI specification that external developers will build on
- Write test coverage that exceeds 80% on all critical paths and mentor future engineers in testing discipline
- Review pull requests from open-source community contributors and maintain code quality standards
- Configure CI/CD pipelines, monitoring, alerting, and on-call rotation with escalation procedures
- Partner with the UX Designer to implement accessible, performant user interfaces in React/Next.js
- Participate in customer discovery calls to understand the workflows of legal aid organizations firsthand
- Be a public technical voice for Justice OS: write technical blog posts, speak at conferences, engage on GitHub

### Requirements
**Technical:**
- 4+ years professional experience with React or Next.js (TypeScript strongly preferred)
- 4+ years with Node.js or similar server-side JavaScript/TypeScript framework
- Proficiency with PostgreSQL: schema design, query optimization, migrations
- Experience with Docker and container orchestration (Kubernetes or equivalent)
- Familiarity with cloud infrastructure (AWS, GCP, or Azure) — infrastructure-as-code preferred
- Experience building and documenting REST APIs (OpenAPI specification)
- Solid understanding of web security: OWASP Top 10, authentication protocols (OAuth2, SAML)

**Mission:**
- Genuine commitment to access to justice, civil legal aid, or civic technology
- Ability to communicate technical decisions to non-technical stakeholders
- Experience contributing to open-source projects (preferred, not required)

### Compensation
- **Base Salary:** $90,000–$120,000 (band based on experience; published and non-negotiable down from midpoint)
- **Equity:** 0.50–1.00% (4-year vest, 1-year cliff, standard ISO options)
- **Benefits:** Health/dental/vision (100% employer-paid employee premium), $2,000 annual learning budget, home office stipend $1,500, 4 weeks PTO
- **Location:** Remote (US-based; must overlap 4+ hours with US Eastern or Central time)

### Success KPIs (First 12 Months)
1. **Shipping Velocity:** Average 2+ features shipped per bi-weekly sprint with <10% rollback rate
2. **Code Quality:** Test coverage ≥80% on all new code; zero P0 bugs introduced in production per quarter
3. **API Performance:** All REST API endpoints maintain p95 response time <500ms at 1,000 concurrent users
4. **System Reliability:** Platform uptime ≥99.5% during the engineer's on-call rotation
5. **Peer Ratings:** Quarterly 360 peer reviews average ≥4.0/5.0, particularly on collaboration and code review quality

### Hiring Strategy
- **GitHub Jobs** — most open-source-leaning engineers see roles here first
- **Hacker News "Who is Hiring"** posts — strong signal for engineers seeking mission-driven work
- **Legal tech Slack communities** — particularly the Legal Hackers and Pro Bono Net networks
- **Open-source contributor pipeline** — monitor Justice OS GitHub contributors for candidates who have already shown product alignment
- **Referrals from civic tech community** — Code for America alumni network, 18F alumni, TechCongress fellows

### Interview Process
1. **Application Review:** Resume + brief cover note on "what does access to justice mean to you?" (not a formal essay)
2. **Intro Call (30 min):** Founder + candidate; focus on motivation, experience highlights, questions from candidate
3. **Take-Home Project (4–6 hours):** Build a simple legal intake form API — given schema requirements and user stories, implement and document; evaluated on code quality, testing, documentation, and design decisions
4. **Technical Interview (90 min):** System design discussion (design a multi-tenant legal case management API); pair programming exercise reviewing their take-home code
5. **Mission & Culture Interview (45 min):** Panel with founder + early advisor; discuss their connection to access-to-justice, conflict resolution style, and approach to ambiguity
6. **Offer:** Issued within 5 business days of final interview

## 5. Month 0–3: UX Designer

### Job Description
Justice OS is seeking a UX Designer who believes beautiful, accessible design is a civil rights issue. You will design the interfaces used by overwhelmed intake workers, frightened self-represented litigants, and under-resourced court clerks. Your work will determine whether people who need legal help can actually get it, or whether they give up because the interface was too confusing. This is one of the most impactful design roles in civic technology.

### Responsibilities
- Lead user research with legal aid staff, self-represented litigants, and court clerks — including low-income users, non-English speakers, and users with disabilities
- Design and maintain the Justice OS design system: components, patterns, tokens, and documentation
- Create high-fidelity prototypes for testing and developer handoff using Figma
- Champion WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility in every design decision and conduct regular accessibility audits
- Collaborate with engineers to implement designs accurately, providing redlines and interaction specifications
- Design onboarding flows that require zero training for self-represented litigants and minimal training for legal aid staff
- Conduct usability testing sessions with real users (not just staff) before each major feature launch
- Create data visualizations for reporting and analytics features that are clear to non-technical executive directors
- Design print-ready and email-format versions of court documents and notices
- Contribute to the open-source design system with documentation that enables community contributors

### Requirements
- 3+ years of product design experience in software or web applications
- Expert proficiency in Figma (components, variants, prototyping, dev mode)
- Strong user research skills: interview facilitation, usability testing, synthesis, and user journey mapping
- Deep knowledge of WCAG 2.1 accessibility guidelines and practical remediation
- Experience designing for users with limited technology literacy or limited English proficiency
- Legal, nonprofit, or civic technology domain experience strongly preferred
- Portfolio must include examples of research-driven design decisions and before/after iterations

### Compensation
- **Base Salary:** $80,000–$100,000
- **Equity:** 0.25–0.50% (4-year vest, 1-year cliff)
- **Benefits:** Same as engineering; additional $500 annual design tools budget

### Success KPIs (First 12 Months)
1. **Task Completion Rate:** Self-represented litigants complete core document workflows at ≥85% completion rate in usability testing
2. **Accessibility Scores:** All new features pass axe-core automated scan with zero critical violations before shipping
3. **User Satisfaction:** Post-onboarding NPS from new users ≥40 (attributable to UX quality)
4. **Design Velocity:** All features enter development with complete Figma specs and no ambiguous design decisions within sprint cycle
5. **Design System:** Design system covers ≥90% of UI patterns in use; documented and used by all contributors

### Hiring Strategy
- Dribbble and Behance — search for portfolios showing accessibility-conscious, user-research-driven work
- AIGA job board — design professionals with strong ethics and social responsibility orientation
- Legal aid network referrals — designers who have previously done pro bono work for legal aid organizations
- Civic design communities — Civic Design community, Code for America design fellows
- College of Legal Informatics programs — emerging field producing designers focused on legal tech

### Interview Process
1. **Application Review:** Resume + portfolio (3–5 case studies minimum, each showing research → design → outcome)
2. **Intro Call (30 min):** Recruiter + candidate; portfolio walkthrough, motivation
3. **Design Challenge (4–6 hours):** Redesign a real court form (provided) to meet WCAG 2.1 AA and plain-language standards; present in Figma
4. **Design Review Interview (90 min):** Walk through design challenge with founder + engineer; discuss decisions, tradeoffs, accessibility choices
5. **Mission & Research Interview (45 min):** Discuss user research approach; how do you design for users you have never been?
6. **Offer**

## 15. Culture & DEI Commitment

### Our Commitment

Justice OS cannot credibly claim to serve people who have been excluded from the legal system if we exclude those same people from our team. DEI at Justice OS is not a policy — it is a product requirement.

### Representation Goals
- **By Month 12:** Women and non-binary employees ≥40% of team; people of color ≥40% of team
- **By Month 18:** Engineering team reflects diversity of US population across race, gender, and first-generation college graduate status
- **Leadership:** C-suite and senior leadership represent our diversity goals by Month 24

### Pay Equity
- Salary bands are published publicly and applied consistently regardless of negotiation style
- Bi-annual pay equity audit: statistical analysis of compensation by gender and race at each band
- Any disparity >5% from expected mean is investigated and corrected within one quarter
- Equity grants reviewed for parity at each hiring decision

### Inclusive Hiring Practices
- Job descriptions reviewed by diverse panel before posting; gendered language removed (no "rockstar," "ninja")
- Blind resume screening for first-round filtering where possible
- Structured interviews: all candidates for a role answer the same core questions with a standardized rubric
- Interview panel includes at least one person who is not the hiring manager and at least one person from an underrepresented group
- All offers extended within the published band regardless of candidate's previous compensation history
- Reasonable accommodations provided for any candidate who requests them in the hiring process

### Ongoing Inclusion
- Quarterly DEI survey: anonymous, reported to entire company, with public action plan
- Annual compensation equity audit with results shared with full team
- Employee Resource Groups funded from operating budget with executive sponsorship
- Conference speaking and professional development budgets distributed equitably
