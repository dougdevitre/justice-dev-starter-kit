## Media Outreach Strategy

### Approach

Do not blast press releases to undifferentiated lists. Earned media comes from:
1. **Personalized pitches** to journalists who cover your exact beat
2. **Timeliness** — connect your story to a news hook, policy development, or trending topic
3. **Exclusives** — offer first crack at a story to one journalist before going wide
4. **Follow-up** — one personalized follow-up email, 3–5 business days after initial pitch

### Pitch Email Template

**Subject:** Story tip: 80% of family court litigants have no attorney — here's the tech trying to change that

---

Hi [Journalist First Name],

[1-sentence personal note — reference a specific article they wrote that's relevant]

I wanted to flag a story that I think fits your coverage of [beat]: the launch of CoTrackPro, a justice tech platform built specifically for the 80% of family court litigants who navigate the system without an attorney.

The quick version: most legal tech is built for lawyers. Almost nothing is built for the people who need help most — unrepresented litigants in custody cases, domestic violence survivors navigating protection orders, low-income parents in child support hearings. CoTrackPro is the first open-source platform designed explicitly for that population and the organizations that serve them.

Angles that might interest you:
- The access-to-justice gap as a technology failure (not just a funding failure)
- How AI guardrails work in a legal context — what happens when AI gives bad legal advice?
- Open source as a justice strategy — why Justice OS is free and what that means for equity

Happy to provide demo access, data, or connect you with legal aid organizations using the platform.

Thanks,  
Doug Devitre
