### Speaking Topics

#### 1. Building Justice Tech That Actually Works
*For developers and technical leaders who want to build in high-stakes, compliance-heavy contexts*

How do you build software for people who can't afford for it to fail? This talk covers the hard engineering lessons from CoTrackPro: HIPAA/FERPA compliance at the data layer, AI guardrails for legal contexts, role-based access for 35+ role types, and trauma-informed UX for users in crisis.

**Best for:** Tech conferences, developer conferences, legal tech, health tech, gov tech  
**Format:** 30–60 min keynote or breakout  
**Audience size:** Any

#### 2. AI Guardrails for High-Stakes Contexts
*For AI practitioners and ethics-focused audiences*

An AI hallucination in a recipe app is annoying. In a custody case, it can cost a parent their child. This talk dives into CoTrackPro's guardrail architecture: citation validation, bias detection, confidence scoring, and forced human review pathways — and what responsible AI in legal contexts actually looks like.

**Best for:** AI/ML conferences, ethics-focused tech conferences, legal tech  
**Format:** 45–60 min keynote or panel  
**Audience size:** Any

#### 3. The 80% Problem: Redesigning Justice for the Unrepresented
*For policy, justice, and social impact audiences*

What happens to the 80% of family court litigants without an attorney? This talk unpacks the access-to-justice crisis through data, story, and technology — and explores what a tech-forward approach to closing the gap actually looks like.

**Best for:** Legal aid, justice conferences, nonprofit tech, policy forums  
**Format:** 30–45 min keynote  
**Audience size:** Any

#### 4. Open Source as a Social Justice Strategy
*For open-source, nonprofit, and founder audiences*

Why is the Justice OS Ecosystem free and open source? This talk explores the philosophy and practical implications of using open source as a tool for equity — how it changes what you build, how you fund it, and who gets to use it.

**Best for:** Open source conferences, nonprofit tech, social impact founder communities  
**Format:** 30–45 min keynote or breakout  
**Audience size:** Any

#### 5. Building for Trust: Compliance, Security, and the Architecture of Accountability
*For enterprise and government technology audiences*

How do you build a system that courts, legal aid organizations, and government agencies will actually trust — and deploy? This talk covers the technical and organizational architecture of trust: HIPAA/FERPA compliance, security design, audit trails, and the procurement realities of selling into regulated environments.

**Best for:** Enterprise tech, government tech, compliance and security conferences  
**Format:** 45–60 min breakout or workshop  
**Audience size:** 20–200
