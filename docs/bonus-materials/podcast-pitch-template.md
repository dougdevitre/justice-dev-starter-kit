## Topic Deep Dive: "Building Justice Tech That Actually Works"

### Hook (Opening 2–3 minutes)

> "Most people think the access-to-justice problem is a money problem. If people just had more legal aid funding, more pro bono attorneys, more courthouse help desks — the gap would close. But here's the thing: we've been throwing money at this for 40 years and 80% of family court litigants still walk in alone. So I started asking: what if it's also a technology problem?"

### Key Talking Points

1. **The 80% stat and what it actually means**  
   It's not just that people can't afford lawyers — it's that the entire system is designed around the assumption that everyone has one. Evidence rules, filing deadlines, courtroom procedures — none of it is accessible to a scared parent trying to keep their family together.

2. **Why existing legal tech doesn't solve it**  
   LegalZoom and Rocket Lawyer are great for simple transactions. Court websites are static PDFs. AI chatbots are hallucinating. None of these were designed for the ongoing, complex, emotionally charged reality of a family court case.

3. **What "trauma-informed design" actually means in code**  
   Every UX decision in CoTrackPro goes through a filter: is this clear enough for someone who hasn't slept in three days and is terrified they're going to lose their child? Plain language. Mobile-first. No jargon. No dark patterns.

4. **AI in legal contexts: the guardrail problem**  
   A bad AI recommendation in a family court case isn't a bug — it's a catastrophe. I'll talk about how CoTrackPro implements citation validation, bias detection, confidence scoring, and mandatory human review pathways to keep AI in its lane.

5. **Open source as a justice strategy**  
   Every workflow template, role configuration, and compliance pattern in Justice OS is free to use, fork, and adapt. Because technology that serves marginalized communities shouldn't be locked behind enterprise license agreements.

### Relevant to Your Audience

*Customize for each show:*

- **Legal tech / law podcasts:** The compliance architecture story — HIPAA, FERPA, role-based access, audit trails
- **AI / developer podcasts:** The guardrails story — how do you build responsible AI for high-stakes contexts?
- **Nonprofit / social impact podcasts:** The access-to-justice story — technology as a lever for equity
- **Founder / startup podcasts:** The open-source business model story — how do you build a sustainable mission-driven company?
- **Government tech podcasts:** The procurement and deployment story — cloud vs. on-prem, compliance, federally-funded billing
