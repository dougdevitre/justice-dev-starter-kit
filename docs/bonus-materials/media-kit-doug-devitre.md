## CoTrackPro — Elevator Pitch

CoTrackPro is a justice tech platform designed for the 80% of family court litigants who navigate the system without an attorney. It provides AI-guided legal workflows, evidence timeline management, court-ready document generation, and HIPAA/FERPA-compliant data handling — all in a single platform built for courts, legal aid organizations, nonprofits, and government agencies.

**In one sentence:** CoTrackPro gives every person in a family court case the tools they need to be heard — regardless of whether they can afford a lawyer.

---

## Key Talking Points

1. **The access-to-justice gap is a technology problem as much as a resource problem.** 80% of family court litigants are unrepresented — not because there are no solutions, but because the tools that exist weren't built for them.

2. **AI in legal contexts requires guardrails, not just models.** CoTrackPro uses AI with citation validation, bias detection, and confidence scoring — because in a family court case, a bad AI answer can cost a parent custody of their child.

3. **Compliance isn't a feature — it's a foundation.** HIPAA and FERPA aren't add-ons in CoTrackPro. They're designed in at the data model level, which is the only way to build trust with courts and government agencies.

4. **Open source is a justice issue.** The Justice OS Ecosystem is open source by design — because technology that serves marginalized communities shouldn't be locked behind enterprise paywalls.

5. **Trauma-informed design matters.** The people using justice tech are often in the worst moments of their lives. Every UX decision in CoTrackPro is made with that reality in mind — clear language, low cognitive load, mobile-first.

6. **The future of legal aid is infrastructure, not apps.** CoTrackPro isn't trying to replace attorneys. It's building the digital infrastructure that makes legal aid 10x more effective.

7. **Justice tech needs builders who understand the system.** Doug Devitre built CoTrackPro after [relevant personal or professional experience] — not as an outsider, but as someone who has seen firsthand what the gap costs.
