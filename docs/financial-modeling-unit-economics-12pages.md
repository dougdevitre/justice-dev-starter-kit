## 1. Financial Overview & Assumptions

Justice OS operates a multi-stream revenue model combining recurring SaaS subscriptions, professional services, and philanthropic grants. This hybrid model is intentional: it allows us to offer free tiers to under-resourced legal aid organizations (funded by grants) while building the commercial revenue base that sustains long-term platform development.

### Business Model Summary
- **SaaS Revenue (primary):** Recurring monthly/annual subscriptions from paying organizations and court systems
- **Services Revenue (secondary):** Implementation, training, and custom development engagements
- **Grants & Donations (supplemental):** Philanthropic grants and government contracts supporting free tier access

### Key Financial Assumptions

| Assumption | Conservative | Base | Optimistic |
|---|---|---|---|
| SaaS customers Year 1 | 8 | 15 | 25 |
| Average Monthly Revenue Per Customer | $350 | $500 | $750 |
| Services utilization rate | 55% | 70% | 80% |
| Annual grant revenue Year 1 | $350K | $500K | $750K |
| Annual customer churn (blended) | 8% | 5% | 3% |
| Sales cycle length (legal aid) | 90 days | 60 days | 45 days |
| Net Revenue Retention | 105% | 110% | 120% |
| Months to operational break-even | 28 | 22 | 16 |

### Scenario Definitions
- **Conservative:** Slower adoption, grant revenue below target, higher churn; requires additional capital raise by Month 18
- **Base:** On-plan growth, grants close on schedule, churn meets targets; extends runway to Month 24 on $1.5M seed
- **Optimistic:** Faster enterprise adoption, grant overperformance, strong NRR; potential to reach profitability by Month 18

## 2. SaaS Unit Economics

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

CAC is calculated as total sales and marketing spend divided by new customers acquired in the period.

| CAC Component | Blended Small Org | Enterprise |
|---|---|---|
| Marketing attribution (content, events, digital) | $800 | $1,500 |
| Sales labor (time-allocated to new business) | $1,500 | $8,000 |
| Onboarding cost (CS time, infrastructure) | $500 | $1,500 |
| **Total CAC** | **$2,800** | **$11,000** |

**Year 1 Blended CAC:** ~$3,200 (skewed toward small orgs in early days)
**Year 3 Blended CAC:** ~$4,500 (enterprise mix increases with VP of Sales team)

### Lifetime Value (LTV)

**Small Organization (Legal Aid):**
- Average Monthly Revenue: $299–$499 → blended ARPU of $400/month
- Average Customer Lifetime: 48 months (5% annual churn = 20-year theoretical lifetime; capped conservatively at 4 years)
- LTV = $400 × 48 = **$19,200**
- Gross Margin: 72% → **LTV (gross) = $13,824**

**Enterprise (Court System / Large Organization):**
- Average Monthly Revenue: $2,999 + $99/user × 15 users = $4,484/month
- Average Customer Lifetime: 60 months (2% annual churn)
- LTV = $4,484 × 60 = **$269,040**
- Gross Margin: 78% → **LTV (gross) = $209,851**

### LTV/CAC Ratio

| Customer Segment | LTV (gross) | CAC | LTV/CAC Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| Small Legal Aid (Year 1) | $13,824 | $2,800 | **4.9:1** |
| Small Legal Aid (Year 3) | $13,824 | $4,200 | **3.3:1** |
| Enterprise (Year 2+) | $209,851 | $11,000 | **19.1:1** |
| Blended (Year 3) | — | — | **~6.2:1** |

**Target:** LTV/CAC > 3:1 at all times. All scenarios exceed this threshold.

### Payback Period

| Segment | Monthly Gross Profit | CAC | Payback Period |
|---|---|---|---|
| Small Legal Aid | $288 (72% × $400) | $2,800 | **9.7 months** |
| Enterprise | $3,498 (78% × $4,484) | $11,000 | **3.1 months** |
| Blended | ~$360 | $3,200 | **~8.9 months** |

### Churn Rate Assumptions

| Customer Type | Annual Churn | Monthly Churn | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|---|
| Legal Aid (Free to Paid) | 5% | 0.42% | Grant-dependent; risk of funding loss |
| Small Private Firm | 8% | 0.67% | More competitive market; lower mission lock-in |
| Court System / Government | 2% | 0.17% | High switching cost; long procurement cycles |
| Enterprise Org | 2% | 0.17% | Deep integration; dedicated CSM |
| **Blended (Year 1)** | **5.5%** | **0.46%** | Skewed toward legal aid |
| **Blended (Year 3)** | **4.0%** | **0.33%** | Enterprise mix increases |

### Net Revenue Retention (NRR) Target

NRR accounts for expansion revenue offsetting churn. Target: **110%+ annually.**

NRR Components:
- Starting ARR: $1,000,000
- Churn: -$50,000 (5%)
- Contraction (downgrades): -$20,000 (2%)
- Expansion (upsells, new users, new modules): +$130,000 (13%)
- **Ending ARR: $1,060,000 → NRR = 106%**

Reaching 110% NRR requires CS team investment in expansion conversations — a core objective of the Day 90 QBR framework.
