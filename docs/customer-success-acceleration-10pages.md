## 1. Overview & CS Philosophy

Customer Success at Justice OS is not a reactive support function — it is a proactive, outcome-driven partnership rooted in our core mission: ensuring that every person, regardless of income, can navigate the legal system with dignity and confidence. We believe that when our customers succeed, justice is served — literally.

### Proactive vs. Reactive CS

| Reactive CS | Justice OS Proactive CS |
|---|---|
| Responds to support tickets | Reaches out before problems surface |
| Measures ticket resolution time | Measures case outcomes and access expanded |
| Focused on product adoption | Focused on mission impact for end clients |
| Quarterly check-ins if remembered | Structured 7/30/60/90-day cadence |
| Renewal conversations at contract end | Expansion conversations at 6-month milestone |

### Our CS Philosophy in Practice

Justice OS Customer Success is built on three pillars:

1. **Outcome-Focused Partnership** — Every onboarding, training, and QBR is anchored to measurable outcomes: cases managed, documents filed, clients served, time saved. We measure what matters to our customers' missions, not just our product metrics.

2. **Mission Alignment** — Our customers are legal aid organizations, courts, and self-represented litigants. Their success is access to justice. We hire CS professionals who understand the weight of that mission and can speak to it credibly with executive directors, staff attorneys, and court clerks alike.

3. **Human-Centered Design** — CS touchpoints are designed around the real constraints of legal aid organizations: lean teams, grant-funded budgets, high caseloads, and technology-skeptical stakeholders. We meet customers where they are, not where we wish they were.

## 2. Pre-Sale Discovery Framework

Effective discovery separates transformational implementations from failed ones. The goal is not to qualify a deal — it is to understand whether Justice OS can genuinely improve outcomes for the organization's clients.

---

### Discovery Call Agenda Template (60 Minutes)

| Time | Segment | Owner | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00–0:05 | Introductions & agenda review | AE + CS | Set context and tone |
| 0:05–0:15 | Their story: mission, clients, geography | Prospect | Understand the organization |
| 0:15–0:25 | Current state: tools, pain points, workflows | AE | Identify gaps |
| 0:25–0:40 | Future state: goals, success criteria, timeline | CS | Define outcomes |
| 0:40–0:50 | Technical environment & IT questions | CS/AE | Qualify technical fit |
| 0:50–0:58 | Next steps and mutual action plan | AE | Advance the deal |
| 0:58–1:00 | Wrap-up and thank you | AE | Close warmly |

---

### 20 Discovery Questions by Category

#### Technical (T)
1. **(T1)** What case management system are you currently using, and how long have you been on it?
2. **(T2)** Do you have an IT team or rely on a managed service provider?
3. **(T3)** What identity provider do you use (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Okta)?
4. **(T4)** How is your client data currently stored, and what format can it be exported in?
5. **(T5)** What is your security review process for new software vendors?

#### Process (P)
6. **(P1)** Walk me through how a new client goes from first contact to case assignment today.
7. **(P2)** Where do cases most commonly get delayed or dropped in your current workflow?
8. **(P3)** How do you currently handle document generation — templates, mail merge, manual drafting?
9. **(P4)** How are court filing deadlines tracked and communicated across your team?
10. **(P5)** How do you handle conflicts of interest checks today?

#### People (Pe)
11. **(Pe1)** Who are the primary users of the system — intake workers, staff attorneys, paralegals?
12. **(Pe2)** Who is the internal champion for this project, and how much executive support do they have?
13. **(Pe3)** How technology-comfortable is your team on a scale of 1–10?
14. **(Pe4)** Have you implemented new software before that failed? What happened?
15. **(Pe5)** How many hours per week does your team currently spend on administrative tasks?

#### Goals (G)
16. **(G1)** What does success look like for you 6 months after go-live?
17. **(G2)** Are there specific grant reporting requirements or outcome metrics you need to hit?
18. **(G3)** What is the single most important problem Justice OS must solve for you?
19. **(G4)** Is there a hard deadline for implementation — a board review, grant period, or fiscal year?
20. **(G5)** What would need to be true for you to say this implementation was a failure?

### Red Flags & Deal Qualification (MEDDIC for Legal Tech)

| MEDDIC Element | Justice OS Question | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|
| **M**etrics | What outcomes will you measure? | "We don't track those numbers" |
| **E**conomic Buyer | Who controls the budget? | No access to decision-maker |
| **D**ecision Criteria | What must be true to sign? | Undefined or shifting criteria |
| **D**ecision Process | How does approval work? | No defined procurement process |
| **I**dentify Pain | What breaks without this? | "Nice to have, not need to have" |
| **C**hampion | Who is fighting for this internally? | No identifiable internal champion |

**Automatic Disqualification Criteria:**
- Organization has <6 months of funding runway
- No IT support and team rates technology comfort <3/10
- No data exportable from current system (migration impossible)
- Board opposition to open-source software with no path to resolution
- Grant deadline requires go-live in <3 weeks (insufficient time for proper onboarding)

### Slide-by-Slide Outline

**Slide 1 — Executive Summary**
- One paragraph: quarter in review, headline wins, headline challenges
- Data needed: most impactful stat, top customer quote, biggest issue resolved

**Slide 2 — Metrics Review**
- Usage metrics: MAU, cases managed, documents generated, logins per week
- Support metrics: tickets opened, avg. resolution time, CSAT score
- Data needed: pull from analytics dashboard 5 days before QBR

**Slide 3 — Progress vs. Original Goals**
- Side-by-side table: agreed goals vs. actual outcomes
- RAG (Red/Amber/Green) status per goal
- Data needed: original success criteria document from onboarding

**Slide 4 — Case Study / Success Story**
- One client story: problem → Justice OS workflow → outcome
- Use anonymized client data, get written approval
- Data needed: 1 nominated story from champion, 1 quantified result

**Slide 5 — Expansion Opportunities**
- What new use cases or departments could benefit from Justice OS?
- Specific features from the roadmap that address their next challenge
- Data needed: usage gaps analysis, feature request log, org growth data

**Slide 6 — Roadmap Preview**
- 3 features shipping in the next quarter most relevant to this customer
- 1 feature they requested and its status
- Data needed: product roadmap, customer feature request log

**Slide 7 — Action Items**
- Table format: Action | Owner | Due Date | Priority
- No more than 5–7 items; each must have a clear owner
- Data needed: collected from discussion throughout meeting

**Slide 8 — Next Quarter Goals**
- 3–5 SMART goals for the coming quarter
- Agreed upon jointly — not dictated by CS
- Data needed: customer's strategic plan for the quarter, CS trend analysis

## 9. Churn Prevention Playbook

### Health Score Model (0–100)

| Component | Weight | Scoring |
|---|---|---|
| Product Usage | 30% | MAU, login frequency, features used vs. licensed |
| Support Ticket Volume & Severity | 20% | Ticket count, P0/P1 issues, unresolved tickets |
| Net Promoter Score (NPS) | 20% | Promoter = 30pts, Passive = 15pts, Detractor = 0pts |
| Contract Renewal Signal | 15% | Months to renewal, renewal conversation status |
| Champion Engagement | 15% | Response time to CS outreach, meeting attendance, referral activity |

**Health Bands:**
- 🟢 **Green (71–100):** Healthy, satisfied, expansion-ready

## 10. NPS Tracking Methodology

### When to Survey

| Survey Moment | Timing | Audience | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Onboarding Complete | Day 28 (end of Week 4) | All new users | Measure onboarding experience |
| 90-Day Check-in | Day 90 | Champion + admin | Validate value realization |
| Recurring | Every 6 months | All users | Track long-term satisfaction trends |
| Post-Support Interaction | 24 hours after ticket close | Ticket submitter | Measure support satisfaction |
| Feature Release | 2 weeks after major release | Active users of feature | Measure feature satisfaction |

---

### Survey Questions

**Q1 (NPS Core):** On a scale of 0–10, how likely are you to recommend Justice OS to a colleague at another legal aid organization or court?

**Q2 (Driver):** What is the most important thing Justice OS does well?

**Q3 (Gap):** What is the one thing Justice OS could improve that would most increase your satisfaction?

**Q4 (Outcome):** Since using Justice OS, has your organization been able to serve more clients? (Yes / No / About the same — brief explanation)

---

### Segmentation Strategy

| Segment | Criteria | Target NPS |
|---|---|---|
| Legal Aid Organizations (<10 staff) | Small community organizations | ≥45 |
| Legal Aid Organizations (10+ staff) | Mid-size legal aid | ≥50 |
| Court Systems | Government buyers | ≥35 |
| Private Firms | Paying SaaS customers | ≥40 |
| Self-Represented Litigants | End users of the platform | ≥55 |

---

### Closing the Loop Process

**Promoters (Score 9–10):**
- Send personal thank-you within 48 hours
- Invite to Customer Advisory Board
- Request case study, testimonial, or conference speaking
- Introduce to referral program

**Passives (Score 7–8):**
- Send follow-up asking: "What would move your score to a 10?"
- Schedule a targeted feature walkthrough within 2 weeks
- Flag for potential expansion conversation if usage supports it

**Detractors (Score 0–6):**
- CS Manager calls within 24 hours — do not email only
- Listen session: 20 minutes, no selling, just understanding
- Escalate to rescue plan if health score is also < 40
- Document learnings and share with product team

---

### NPS Improvement Initiatives

1. **Monthly Product Webinars** — reduce "I didn't know the feature existed" feedback
2. **Peer Learning Community** — connect organizations doing similar work to share best practices
3. **Feature Request Transparency** — publish a public roadmap so customers see their requests addressed
4. **Faster Support SLAs** — reduce P2 resolution from 48 hours to 24 hours to raise support satisfaction
5. **Annual User Conference** — in-person event builds relationship depth that surveys cannot capture
