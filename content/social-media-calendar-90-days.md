### Week 2

**Monday**
- 🐦 **Twitter:** Architecture thread (Principle 1): *"Thread: 5 design principles for justice tech that actually works. Starting with #1: Reduce cognitive load. Your users are not at their best. Here's what that means for your interface design. 🧵"*
- 💼 **LinkedIn:** "This week I'm sharing the 5 principles behind CoTrackPro's 89% completion rate. One per day. Starting with cognitive load:"

**Tuesday**
- 🐦 **Twitter:** Architecture tweet (Principle 2 — Return Control): *"Trauma = loss of control. Your UX should restore it. This means: showing progress, making actions reversible, previewing outcomes, explaining what happens next. 2/5 in my justice tech design thread."*
- 💼 **LinkedIn:** Continue daily principles series (Return Control)

**Wednesday**
- 🐦 **Twitter:** Architecture tweet (Principle 3 — Language): *"Every word in your interface is a design decision. 'Petitioner' vs 'You'. 'Error: Required' vs 'This field needs more info'. 'Are you sure?' vs 'Let's double-check before you submit'. 3/5"*
- 💼 **LinkedIn:** Continue daily principles series (Neutral Language)

**Thursday**
- 🐦 **Twitter:** Architecture tweet (Principle 4 — Accessibility): *"Accessibility is not a feature. It's legal compliance, market access, and ethical design in one. WCAG 2.1 AA is required for any tool touching court systems or federal funding. And it's not that hard to build in from the start. 4/5"*
- 💼 **LinkedIn:** Continue daily principles series (Accessibility)

**Friday**
- 🐦 **Twitter:** Architecture tweet (Principle 5 — Outcome Metrics): *"Vanity metrics for justice tech: users registered. Outcome metrics: documents filed, protective orders granted, cases resolved. If you don't know your completion rate, you don't know if your tool works. 5/5"*
- 💼 **LinkedIn:** Wrap up principles series. CTA to download whitepaper or clone starter kit.

## Monthly Review Process

At the end of each month:
1. Pull analytics from all platforms (Twitter Analytics, LinkedIn Analytics, Google Analytics)
2. Compare against targets in content-distribution-checklist.md
3. Identify top 3 performing pieces of content
4. Identify bottom 3 and adjust approach
5. Update this calendar based on what's working
6. Review and update distribution checklist targets
